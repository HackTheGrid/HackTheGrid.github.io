The Flipper Zero is an incredible multi-tool for hackers and pentesters. This compact device packs a wide array of features, including a sub-GHz radio module capable of interacting with wireless devices. In this post, I'll demonstrate how I used the Flipper Zero to control my garage door.

Objective:

To open and close my garage door using the Flipper Zero instead of the standard remote.

Step-by-Step: Hacking My Garage Door Remote

1. Identify the Signal Frequency

- Open the Frequency Analyzer app on your Flipper Zero.
- Press the button on your garage door remote while holding it near the Flipper.
- The Flipper Zero will display the frequency of the signal. In my case, it was 433.8 MHz.

2. Configure the Flipper for the Target Frequency

- Back out of the Frequency Analyzer and open the Read app.
- Press the left arrow to open Config and set the frequency to the value you found earlier (e.g., 433.8 MHz).

3. Capture the Signal

- With the correct frequency set, press the button on your remote again.
- The Flipper Zero will capture and display the signal in binary format.

4. Transmit the Signal

- Switch to the Transmit app on the Flipper Zero.
- Select the captured signal and transmit it. If successful, your garage door should open or close just like it would with the original remote!

Congratulations!

You’ve successfully hacked a sub-GHz device using the Flipper Zero. It’s amazing how simple and intuitive this little device makes such tasks.

Potential Challenges and Solutions

  • Rolling Codes: Many modern garage doors use rolling codes for security. In this case, a simple signal replay won’t work. You might need to employ more advanced techniques, such as a roll jam attack, to bypass this security feature.
  • Range Limitations: The Flipper Zero’s sub-GHz module has a relatively short range. To improve range, consider attaching an external antenna like the CC1101 module for better performance.
